Hi there!

My name is Tanli Su and I'm from Palo Alto, California. I'm currently a sophomore at Dartmouth College and plan on majoring in math and computer science.

Last summer, I took a course in Full Stack Development while studying abroad in Lisbon, Portugal. I enjoy designing and developing mobile apps because it's exciting to see projects that I've created come to life as beautiful, functioning products!

projects

OPEN ENERGY HUB BACKEND

As part of my study abroad program last summer, I did a short, unpaid internship at BlockLab NL. My internship group developed a PoC blockchain backend system for BlockLab's Open Energy Hub project using JavaScript, Node.js, 3Box, IPFS, and OrbitDB. For this project, I had to do a lot of reading to learn about blockchain technology, distributed databases, and the technologies that we were using to build the backend.

MOCK AIRBNB MOBILE APP

For the final project of my Full Stack class last summer, I built a mobile app inspired by Airbnb. The app allows guests to register an account, login/logout, view listings, and create bookings; all of this information is stored in a MySQL database. For this project, I worked with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, TypeScript, Ionic, Angular, Node.js, Express, Postman, and MySQL.

COMPUTATIONAL DOCKING OF ANTIBIOTIC ENZYMES

In my first year at Dartmouth, I did research as a Women In Science Project intern with Professor Chris Bailey-Kellogg. For my project, I used computational methods (AutoDock Vina, PyMol, Python) to analyze potential antibiotic enzymes against drug-resistant Staph.
